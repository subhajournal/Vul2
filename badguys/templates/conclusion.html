Conclusion

Congratulations on making it through all of the Top Ten vulnerabilities!

Here are a few thoughts we'd like to leave you with...

Think Like a Bad Guy

Your product designer or business owner may be able to think like a regular user, but you don't have that luxury. In order to protect your users, every feature must be considered from the point of view of an attacker trying to gain leverage over your system.

Don't Stop at Ten

These are just the most common vulnerabilities; there's a very scary long tail of other things that can go wrong. Protecting against the Top Ten should be a starting place, not your final destination.

The Only Constant is Change

New vulnerabilities are always being discovered; what you thought was secure yesterday may not be today. The only way to cope with this is to stay on your game and adapt to new challenges as they arise.

Think Positive

This may all sound pretty daunting, but if you keep your wits about you and stay positive, you can make a significant difference. Make a plan and execute on it, even in incremental steps, and you'll be in a much better place before you know it.
